# Trademark & Naming Policy

**The code is open. The name is not a free-for-all.**

Wisp is released under the [MIT License](./LICENSE) — you can read,
run, modify, fork, and build commercial products on the code, with no
permission or payment needed. This document is about one thing the MIT license
does **not** cover: the **name and identity** of the project.

The name "Wisp," any associated logos, and the wording of the
**honesty contract** (the confidence labels and claims table) are held by the
project's author, legitslei. They are what tells users *"this is the tool
that keeps its privacy promise."* The whole point of the project is that the
name means something specific — so the name has to be protected even though the
code is free.

## What you can always do

No permission required:

- **Use** the software, hosted or self-hosted, for anything — including
  commercial use.
- **Modify** any part of the code and run your changed version privately or in
  production.
- **Fork** it and maintain your own version.
- **Redistribute** it (the MIT license just asks you keep the copyright notice).
- **Say your product "uses Wisp"** or **"is built on Wisp,"** as a plain,
  truthful factual statement — as long as you don't imply the
  project endorses or maintains your product.

## What requires a different name

If you distribute a **modified version**, please **rename it.** Don't ship a
fork under the name "Wisp." This protects users: it means anyone who
sees the name is looking at the real project with the promises it actually
keeps — not a fork that may have changed them.

In particular, **you must rename your fork if you change anything that breaks
the honesty contract**, including (but not limited to):

- Adding device fingerprinting, IP logging, or any identifier collection.
- Storing or transmitting emails, phone numbers, or other personal data.
- Adding new confidence labels, or making stronger claims than the three the
  project returns (`bot-filtered`, `same-passkey return`, `same-code return`).
- Any "guaranteed unique human" or cross-site tracking claim.

These are exactly the things the project promises *not* to do
(see the "What's deliberately NOT here" section of the [README](./README.md)).
A version that does them is a different product and needs a different name.

## What you may not do

- **Don't use the name or logo in a way that implies endorsement,**
  affiliation, or official status ("official," "certified," "powered by the
  Wisp team") without written permission.
- **Don't use the name for your company, product, domain, or social account**
  in a way that could confuse users into thinking it's the official project.
- **Don't remove or alter the copyright and license notices** in copies you
  distribute (this one is also a license requirement).

## The simple version

> **The code is yours to use and change. The name stays honest.**
> If your version still keeps every promise in the README, you're welcome to
> credit the project by name. If it changes the promises, give it your own name.
